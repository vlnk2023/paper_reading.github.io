Case 2: α = 100 and β = 0.01

This distribution corresponds to the more concentrated PDF in Figure A.1. In this case, the mean and variance are αβ = 1 and αβ2 = 0.01, respectively, so the coefficient of variation is 10%. This random variable has the same mean as Case 1 but a smaller variance. Figure A.3 shows the Hessian and the estimated variance using different finite-difference step sizes. Again, the estimation depends on the step size. The correct value of the variance is 0.01 and the error of the estimation is about 1% when the step size is small. In general, the finite-difference estimated variance is more accurate for a smaller coefficient of variation of the distribution because the local topology of the PDF is more representative for the global distribution in this case. This example shows that this approximation is acceptable up to 10% COV.
